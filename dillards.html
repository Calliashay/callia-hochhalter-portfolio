Project Overview

This project involved a comprehensive audit of regional retail transaction data to improve profitability and identify operational risks. The analysis focused specifically on Store 917 in Casa Grande, Arizona, selected for its unique performance metrics relative to state-wide volume averages.

Technical Approach

The audit was executed through a multi-stage data pipeline to ensure accuracy and actionable results:

  • Data Extraction & Cleaning: Used Python to process raw transaction logs, handling over 122,000 individual records to normalize sales data across 1,114 days of history.
  • KPI Benchmarking: Established performance baselines for net sales and transaction volume to compare Store 917 against regional averages.
  • Trend Analysis: Leveraged PowerBI to identify seasonal spikes in high-value categories, such as designer handbags and beauty products.
  • Risk Identification: Developed custom scripts to flag statistical outliers in return behavior, isolating potential fraud and systemic POS errors.

Core Findings & Strategic Recommendations

  • Handbag Performance: Sales for Michael Kors and Brahmin peak significantly in December and May.
    INVENTORY Action: Prioritize stock of Black, Brown, and Vanilla colorways during peak windows to capture the highest brand-specific demand.
  • Gift Card Utility: Gift card transactions correlate with a higher overall "basket size" compared to standard payment methods.
    MARKETING Action: Launch targeted "Spend $80, Get $100" promotions in December to drive volume in premium beauty categories.
  • Inventory Calibration: Home and Decor categories represent only 11.55% of sales but drive 33.54% of store discounts.
    PROFITABILITY Action: Phase out underperforming cutlery lines to protect department margins from excessive discounting.
  • Fraud Mitigation: Flagged a specific outlier returning 123% of their total purchase value.
    RISK CONTROL Action: Implement manual audit flags for any customer account exceeding a 40% return-to-purchase ratio.
