ADA Compliance & User Flow Simplification.

Design & Discovery

The redesign began with a deep dive into user needs, specifically focusing on the frustrations of the site operator and the unique requirements of a senior demographic.

Platform Feasibility: Built a matrix comparing Wix, WordPress, and Squarespace across operational, economic, and technical criteria. Wix was selected for its superior drag-and-drop interface and long-term ease of maintenance.
Inclusive UI/UX: Prioritized ADA compliance as a central design pillar, specifically optimizing font sizes, color contrast, and simplified navigation for seniors.

Agile Implementation

To ensure the best outcome for the client, the project management style shifted from a rigid Waterfall methodology to an Agile approach.

  • Iterative Feedback: Successive iterations focused on the evolving needs of the client rather than strict process control.
  • Refined Layouts: This flexibility allowed for collaborative refinements to the layout that traditional methods might have overlooked.
  • Key Deliverables: Produced a comprehensive suite of documentation including a Feasibility Study, Data Collection Plan, and full Design Document.

Recommendations & Future Growth

Post-implementation recommendations focused on digitizing the center's operational workflows:

  • Phasing MSC membership and volunteer applications into digital submission forms.
  • Enhanced integration with Missoula Aging Services.
  • Strategic updates to the Thrift Store presence to showcase current inventory.
